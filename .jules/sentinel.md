## 2025-01-24 - Harden FRI verifier against DoS and soundness issues
**Vulnerability:** The FRI verifier (two-adic and circle) relied on prover-supplied metadata (log_arities, schedule length) to calculate domain heights and allocate memory. An adversarial prover could trigger panics by exceeding field limits (e.g., TWO_ADICITY), cause DoS by requesting massive arity-based allocations, or trigger arithmetic underflow by providing fewer rounds than expected by the verifier's input matrices.
**Learning:** Verifiers must always validate proof-controlled metadata against verifier-expected bounds and field/system constants before using them in arithmetic (especially shifts and subtractions) or allocations.
**Prevention:** Use `checked_add` for reduction sums, validate `log_arity > 0` and `log_arity <= max_log_arity`, ensure `log_global_max_height` is within field limits, and strictly enforce that the prover's reduction schedule results in the height expected by the verifier's input matrices.
